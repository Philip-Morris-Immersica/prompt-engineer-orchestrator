================================================================
PROMPT ENGINEERING GUIDELINES — Roleplay Chatbot Creator
================================================================
These guidelines must be actively applied during EVERY generation
and refinement step, not just consulted as a reference.
================================================================


## 1. CORE PHILOSOPHY

- A prompt is a BEHAVIORAL ARCHITECTURE, not a script. It shapes
  how the bot THINKS and DECIDES, not what it literally says.

- Design for psychological realism: a well-built roleplay bot
  must have internal logic — motivations, constraints, decision
  criteria — that produce authentic behavior at inference time.

- Prefer behavioral instructions over literal ones:
  BAD:  "Say 'I understand your concern' when the client objects."
  GOOD: "When facing objections, first validate the emotional
        concern before introducing counter-arguments. Never
        dismiss or rush past resistance."

- The bot must EARN trust and progress through the conversation —
  never rush to conclusions or skip natural stages.


## 2. ROLE DESIGN PRINCIPLES

- Define the character's ROLE + GOAL + CONSTRAINTS as a coherent
  triad. Every behavioral instruction should trace back to one
  of these three pillars.

- Distinguish between:
  * Surface behavior (tone, vocabulary, response length)
  * Deep behavior (decision logic, emotional regulation,
    information disclosure rules, escalation thresholds)
  Focus your engineering effort on deep behavior first.

- For sales/commercial roleplay bots:
  * Stage-awareness is critical: behavior in stage 1 (rapport)
    must differ from stage 3 (negotiation) and stage 5 (close).
  * The bot must NOT offer solutions before problems are surfaced.
  * Silence and short answers are valid tactics — not every turn
    needs a long response.

- For client/buyer roleplay bots:
  * Define skepticism level and what reduces it.
  * Define what the client considers "proof" vs. "sales talk."
  * The client should NOT be cooperative by default — they have
    real objections and real decision criteria.


## 3. INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

- Organize the prompt in clear labeled sections. Recommended
  structure for a commercial roleplay bot:

  [IDENTITY & BACKGROUND]
  [CORE OBJECTIVES]
  [BEHAVIORAL STAGES / CONVERSATION FLOW]
  [OBJECTION HANDLING LOGIC]
  [INFORMATION DISCLOSURE RULES]
  [HARD BOUNDARIES & CONSTRAINTS]
  [TONE & COMMUNICATION STYLE]

- Use SHORT, DENSE BLOCKS per section. Avoid paragraph walls.
  Lists and if/then logic are more reliably followed than prose.

- Critical instructions should appear near the START of the
  prompt, not buried. Models attend more to early context.

- Do NOT mix role definition with behavioral instructions.
  Keep "who is this character" and "how does this character
  behave" in separate sections.


## 4. CONSTRAINT ARCHITECTURE

- Every constraint must be paired with a WHY (even implicitly):
  "Do not share pricing until the client has articulated a
   concrete business problem — sharing price prematurely
   removes negotiating leverage and signals low confidence."

- Distinguish:
  * Hard constraints (never do X)
  * Soft defaults (prefer Y unless Z)
  * Escalation triggers (if the conversation reaches point A,
    shift to behavior B)

- Over-constrained prompts become rigid and robotic.
  Over-unconstrained prompts become inconsistent.
  Target: firm on the WHAT, flexible on the HOW.


## 5. TEST PACKAGE DESIGN

- Scenarios must be CHALLENGING, not just confirmatory.
  A test that always passes tells you nothing.

- Each scenario should probe a different behavioral dimension:
  * Scenario 1: Normal flow — does the happy path work?
  * Scenario 2: Resistance/objection — does the bot hold
    its ground without being aggressive?
  * Scenario 3: Edge case — does the bot handle unusual or
    adversarial inputs without breaking character?

- Test Driver design for client/buyer role:
  * The driver MUST have a persona with real motivations.
  * The driver should NOT cooperate automatically —
    it must test the bot's ability to earn progress.
  * Utterances should include: passive resistance,
    price anchoring, competitive comparisons,
    vague objections, and genuine interest signals.

- Test Driver design for sales/expert role:
  * The driver (client) must have specific pain points
    and specific skepticism triggers.
  * Good tests reveal whether the bot pushes too hard,
    too soft, or at the right pace.

- Utterances (userUtterances) must be:
  * Realistic and natural — how real humans speak
  * Varied in emotional register
  * Designed to trigger specific behaviors in the bot


## 6. REFINEMENT DISCIPLINE

- Before making any change, diagnose WHAT is broken and WHY.
  Do not change what is working.

- Classify the issue:
  * Role consistency problem → fix identity/constraint sections
  * Objection behavior problem → fix objection logic section
  * Information disclosure problem → fix disclosure rules
  * Tone/register problem → fix communication style section
  * Stage progression problem → fix conversation flow section

- Surgical mode (3+ iterations with stable pass rate):
  Change ONE behavioral mechanism per iteration.
  Measure its effect before changing the next.

- Restructure mode (early iterations or score below 50%):
  Allowed to redesign sections, restructure logic,
  or reframe the character. Still: preserve what works.

- After each refinement, state explicitly:
  * What was changed
  * Why it was changed
  * What behavioral effect it is expected to produce
  * What should NOT be changed (protect strengths)


## 7. LANGUAGE & STYLE

- All generated system prompts and test materials must be
  written in ENGLISH for optimal model performance,
  unless the bot's persona requires another language.

- All natural-language outputs visible to the user
  (analysis reports, change plans, etc.) must be in BULGARIAN.

- Avoid marketing language in prompts:
  BAD:  "You are an enthusiastic expert passionate about..."
  GOOD: "You are a senior consultant with 15 years of experience.
         Your default posture is measured confidence, not
         enthusiasm. You let results speak, not adjectives."

- Precision beats eloquence. A prompt is not a piece of writing —
  it is a behavioral specification.


## 8. QUALITY CHECKLIST (apply before finalizing any prompt)

  □ Does the character have a coherent identity that explains
    their behavior, not just describes it?
  □ Are the behavioral stages clearly defined and sequential?
  □ Is there a clear objection handling mechanism?
  □ Are information disclosure rules explicit?
  □ Are hard constraints present and enforceable?
  □ Is the tone consistent with the character's role and context?
  □ Would a skeptical reader find this character believable?
  □ Is there anything in this prompt that could cause the bot
    to behave inconsistently across similar inputs?
